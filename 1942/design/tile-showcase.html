🎮 1942 Tile Set Showcase

Comparing 4 different approaches to 64x64 pixel tiles for the Coral Front campaign.
Spec: Water (deep/shallow), terrain (sand/grass), edge pieces, clouds.

ALPHA: Clean & Geometric

Tech Architect + Miyamoto collaboration
Focus: Sharp, clean pixel art with clear geometric shapes and high contrast

Individual Tiles

12x12 Level Sample

Alpha Results: Dual-track strategy with programmatic tiles (guaranteed edge matching) + AI enhancement. Clean geometric precision optimized for readability. Recommended for production due to pixel-perfect seaming and Miyamoto's accessibility focus.

BETA: Organic & Textured

Tech Architect + Kojima collaboration
Focus: Natural, organic shapes with texture and atmospheric depth

Individual Tiles

12x12 Level Sample

Beta Results: Most comprehensive set (23 tiles) using procedural Perlin noise generation. Kojima's atmospheric vision with organic textures and natural depth. Shallow water/foam tiles are standouts. Some corner seam alignment issues noted.

GAMMA: Retro Arcade Classic

Tech Architect + Ikeda collaboration
Focus: Authentic 80s/90s arcade pixel art with limited color palettes

Individual Tiles

12x12 Level Sample

Gamma Results: Hardcore retro authenticity with NES-era constraints (strict 4-5 color palettes, no anti-aliasing, structured dithering). Includes both outer AND inner corner pieces. L-shaped island with detached reef shows classic shmup design sensibility.

DELTA: Minimalist & Stylized

Tech Architect + Okamoto collaboration
Focus: Simple, bold shapes with modern indie game aesthetics

Individual Tiles

12x12 Level Sample

Delta Results: Clean minimalist approach with modern indie aesthetics. Excellent color separation and readability at scroll speed. Strong tropical island formation. Note: Generated tiles need alpha transparency for proper cloud overlay integration.

🎯 Evaluation Criteria

  • Visual Cohesion: Do tiles work well together as a set?
  • Readability: Can players easily distinguish terrain types during gameplay?
  • Performance: How well will these compress and tile in WebGL?
  • Scalability: How well will this style work for other campaigns?
  • Feel: Does it capture the right arcade shooter vibe?
